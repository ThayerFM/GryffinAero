Heinkel He.18

This is a preliminary page to park some progress photos and a few thoughts about my latest Free Flight project, the Heinkel He.18. This rubber-powered model will have a 54-inch wing span, and is based on a much smaller electric design by Hurst Bowers, published years ago in Model Aviation.

The He.18 was introduced in Germany in 1925 for sport flying and flight school use. The fuselage structure was welded from steel tube, though a wood version was also available for those without local access to skilled metal workers for maintenance. Wings were wood construction, and folded along the fuselage for storage in a small garage. It could be towed to the owner's home with his automobile, or even a motorcycle.

The full scale version was powered with a 50 or 75 hp Siemens radial engine, depending on your budget, and could be fitted with either wheels or floats.
