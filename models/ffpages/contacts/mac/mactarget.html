Target Time

Marin Aero Club often will contest a "Target Time" contest. The rules are simple. After trimming your model, declare a specific target time over 20 seconds. Then commence with 3 official flights, recording each flight's time on the timing sheet. Whoever has the least total deviation from their target time is declared the winner.

This format is a great equalizer in that consistency is the key, not outright duration. It also allows models that might be a bit heavy to go head to head with, and often beat, their lighter cousins. In 1997 this contest was won with a total error of 1 second over 3 flights. A declared time of 25 seconds was flown with times of 25, 26, 25. Truly an amazing performance. The model? A 'stock from the box' EasyBuilt Stinson Reliant Peanut.
