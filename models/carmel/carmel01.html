Carmel-by-the-Sea

21 October 2001

a day on the beach

Staking our claim at 8 am.
Amazingly, we were the first to start.

Jeff and Anne after consulting with the judges on a "rule clarification".

Rachel fortifying for the day with Gatorade.

While Anne prepares to begin ...

... Rachel starts the water.

A practice run determines undercutting potential.

Rachel keeps the water coming while the rest of us work on the foundation.

Starting the main tower at about 9:30 am.
Note the position of the team behind us.

Because of limited mobility, sections of the tower were carved by feel.

A Sand Marshall wonders "How did they do that?"
He was not alone this day. We were just as surprised.

The team behind us misread the tide tables and had to
relocate further up the beach slope, losing much valuable time.

Finished with the "Peasant's Village", Rachel moves around front to the courtyards.

I decide to add a thatched Gatekeeper's Cottage.

"Boar's Glade".

The Judges frolic in the moat ...

... and pose for a picture

Now it is our turn. Honest! We ARE standing!

The Service Entrance, Gatekeeper's Tower and the Thatched Cottage.

Fortune smiles upons us and we are awarded the "Best Traditional Sand Castle".

Boar's Glade & the Peasant's Village.

The Courtyard Tower and Ruin.

Still standing 5 1/2 hrs after carving the main tower.

One last look as we leave.
